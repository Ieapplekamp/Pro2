Projects

Little Stitious, developed to be a "Truther's" go to place for all things cryptic! The idea was to create a space for people to be able to post about all the unexplained things they've whitnessed, from ghosts to ghouls. Users can find the most updated information about siting, as well as post their own to the database.

For this project my main priorities were user authentication and design.
Order Up! is a crud app, built around Handlebars and MySQL. Users can update and delete orders, much like a "to do" list. Other libraries used for this project are, AnimateCSS and jQuery.

This was one of the more challenging assigments, as those two technologies were a bit of a hassle to deploy to Heroku and the file structure for Handlebars was initially a challenge to wrap my head around.
Friend Finder is a full-stack application, using Express, NodeJS, HTML5, Bootstrap, and Sweet Alert. The app matches the user with the most compatible 'friend' based on their responses to the survey.

The theme of this app was based off of the Scary Movie trilogy. This was one of my more favorite projects, because I was able to have fun and make it about something I found humorous.

About

My name is Ian Applekamp, En Apple. I'm 23, from the southern part of Wisconsin. I'm currently a student of the UNC coding bootcamp for full-stack web development, looking to transition my skills into more Front-End UI/UX design, after graduation. My background doesn't include much coding experience, unless editing Myspace and Tumblr layouts in middle school and high school counts.

However, in March of 2019, I decided to quit my job and fully immerse myself into the world of programming. Initially, I believed I could have been a self taught developer... but then I really started to get into JavaScript.. That's when I knew had bitten off more than I could chew. Not looking to give up, I enrolled into a coding bootcamp. Not only to get a better understanding of the languages and concepts, but to go further as a future developer.
